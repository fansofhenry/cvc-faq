Class begins in 48 hours and I'm still Pending
Exact steps before you panic-email
  1. Check your CVC inbox at search.cvc.edu after login. The Teaching College may have requested a document. If they did, you have to respond before they can finish.
  2. Confirm the status hasn't changed. "Pending Person Match" and "Prerequisite Pending Review" both look stuck — but they mean the Teaching College is actively reviewing you. They are not denied.
  3. Email support with the words "URGENT — CLASS START [date]" in the subject line, plus the information from the ticket builder below. Replies on time-sensitive tickets are typically same-day.
  4. While you wait, identify a backup section at search.cvc.edu. If the original Teaching College can't move fast enough, you may need to register for a different open section.
My drop deadline is today or tomorrow
What to do if drop fails or you're stuck
  1. Try the drop one time. Don't retry repeatedly — that doesn't help, and it creates duplicate records that slow resolution.
  2. Take a screenshot of any error. The exact error text determines what kind of fix is needed.
  3. Email support immediately with subject "URGENT — DROP DEADLINE [date]" and your CCCID, course/CRN, and screenshot. Use the ticket builder to format it correctly.
  4. Email the Teaching College's Admissions & Records office in parallel. Your email is documented intent to drop before the deadline. If anything goes wrong on our end, that record protects you. Find the A&R email at cvc.edu/students/refunds.
Note. Drop deadlines are per-section, not per-term. Look up your specific date on the Teaching College's deadline tool — don't assume the standard semester schedule.
My card was charged but I'm not enrolled
Almost always the missing Confirm Payment click
  1. Log in to search.cvc.edu. Look at your Class Schedule. Does it say "Pay Now" or "Enrolled & Paid"?
  2. If it says "Pay Now": the system never received your payment confirmation. The fix is on our side; we can reconcile it. Email support with your CCCID, course, payment date/time, last 4 of card, and amount.
  3. If it says "Enrolled & Paid": you're fine — the dashboard caught up. Done.
  4. Don't pay again. A second payment goes to the same place and creates a refund headache, not enrollment.
Why this happens. Students close the browser after the bank confirms the charge — but before clicking the Confirm Payment button on the page CVC redirects you to. Always click Confirm Payment.

iii.

Decoding the error messages

Click any entry to see the real meaning, the realistic timeline, and what (if anything) you should do. Italics indicate wait — this is correct system behavior.

Pending Person Match
wait
What it means
The Teaching College needs to match your CCCID to a record in their system. A normal step — not a failure.
Whose ball
The Teaching College's. CVC has handed it to them.
Realistic timeline
1–3 business days at most colleges. Five or more days is unusual.
When to email support
After 5 business days, or if class starts within 48 hours.
Action now — nothing. Don't resubmit; don't email yet. Use the date checker to confirm exactly when to follow up.
Prerequisite Pending Review
wait
What it means
Teaching College's A&R is reviewing your prerequisite documents. You are not denied — you are waiting.
Whose ball
The Teaching College's A&R office.
Timeline
3–5 business days for CCC prereqs. 5–7 for non-CCC (private, four-year, or out-of-state).
When to email
After the timeline above, or if class starts within 48 hours.
Critical — do not resubmit while waiting. Duplicate submissions create conflicting records and slow the review. One application at a time.
Course Filled / Class is full
move on
What it means
The section reached capacity before your registration completed.
Common misread
Students often think their prereqs were rejected. They weren't — the system never got that far. The seat check happens first.
Waitlist?
No. The CVC Exchange has no waitlists or add codes. Once full, you can't queue.
Email support?
No — there's no fix. This is correct system behavior.
Three options — (i) find another open section at any Teaching College via search.cvc.edu, (ii) apply directly to the Teaching College outside CVC at icangotocollege.com — they may have their own waitlist, (iii) take the course in a future term.
Registration Failed (no specific message)
check detail
What it means
"Registration Failed" is a category, not a specific cause. The actual reason lives in the Application Errors field on your enrollment record.
Where to look
CVC dashboard → Class Schedule → click the failed enrollment → read the error message exactly.
Action — match the exact error text against this decoder. If it mentions "Course Filled," "prerequisite," "residency," or "hold," see the matching entry. If it's something else, copy the exact text and email support.
Drop Failed
email — once
What it means
The drop didn't process. Possible reasons: a hold on your account, a passed deadline, or a system error on our side.
Don't retry
Repeat attempts won't fix it and create duplicate records that slow resolution.
Time pressure
If your drop deadline is within 72 hours, mark the email URGENT.
Email once — with CCCID, course/CRN, screenshot of the error, and your drop deadline date. Use the ticket builder.
Application Denied
read inbox first
What it means
Someone at the Teaching College reviewed your application and declined it. Different from "Registration Failed" (a system error).
Common reasons
Prerequisite not accepted, residency status, eligibility (GPA or holds), capacity at the program level.
First step
Check your CVC inbox — most colleges send a denial reason there.
If no reason given — email support so we can ask the college on your behalf. Don't keep resubmitting; that re-triggers the same denial.
Status Update Failed (on a class you dropped)
probably nothing
What it usually means
A display issue. The drop actually succeeded at the Teaching College; the dashboard just shows a stale state.
Don't re-attempt
Re-dropping a class that's already dropped creates problems.
How to verify
Email support with your CCCID and course — we can confirm the real state with the Teaching College in 1 business day.
Tuition shows $400+ for 3 units
don't pay
What it means
Your California residency wasn't confirmed at the Teaching College, so the system is charging you out-of-state tuition.
In-state rate
$46 per unit. $138 for 3 units. $184 for 4. If you see anywhere near 3–9× that, it's a residency issue.
Whose fix
The Teaching College's Admissions & Records office. They update the residency flag in their system.
Do not pay. Email the Teaching College's A&R office and ask them to confirm your CA residency on file. Tuition recalculates after they fix it. If you've already paid, email CVC support — refunding overpayments takes weeks.
Card charged but dashboard says "Pay Now"
recoverable
What happened
The bank processed the charge, but you didn't click Confirm Payment on the CVC page that opened after. The Teaching College never got the "paid" signal.
Don't pay twice
A second payment goes to the same place. It doesn't fix the first; it just creates a refund problem.
Fix timeline
Support reconciles these in 1–2 business days.
Email once — with CCCID, course/CRN, payment date and time, last 4 of card, and amount charged. Use the ticket builder. We do the rest.
Class doesn't show up in Canvas
wait — usually timing
Normal timeline
24–48 hours after registration. Some colleges only provision Canvas closer to class start.
Where to look
Your Home College's Canvas, not the Teaching College's. Click the link from CVC dashboard → Class Schedule. In Canvas, click "View All Courses" if it's not on the dashboard.
Login error
"Trust relationship" or SSO errors are college IT issues. Try a different browser or incognito mode first.
When to email
After 3 business days, or within 24 hours of class start.
I don't know what I'm looking at
walk through it

Open search.cvc.edu, log in, and look at your Class Schedule. For each course, you'll see a status. Match it below:

  • —Validated & Registered — you're enrolled. Done.
  • —Enrolled & Paid — also done.
  • —Pay Now — payment didn't go through. See "charged but unpaid" if you already tried.
  • —Pending anything — system is still working. Check the date tool.
  • —Failed / Denied — see the error decoder above.

v.

Eight common mistakes

If you recognize one of these in what you did, the fix is in the entry. No ticket needed.

Closed the browser after paying

Fix — see "charged but unpaid". Don't pay again. Email once with payment evidence.

Resubmitted while a status was Pending

Fix — only one application per course at a time. Duplicate submissions confuse the review. Wait for the existing one to resolve before submitting again.

Dropped the wrong class first, then registered for the right one

Fix — the order matters. Always register for the new class first (locks the seat), pay, then drop the old one. Money does not transfer between classes.

Paid the out-of-state tuition because it looked correct

Fix — in-state is $46 per unit. If you saw $400 or more for 3 units and paid, email CVC support with payment evidence. Refunds take weeks. Always verify residency before paying.

Tried to find class in the Teaching College's Canvas

Fix — always use your Home College's Canvas; that's how the trust relationship works. The Teaching College's Canvas won't show your CVC enrollment directly.

Emailed support six hours after a "Pending" status

Fix — wait 3–5 business days for normal Pending; 5–7 for non-CCC prereqs. Use the date tool to confirm. Premature emails just mean the same answer ("still processing") sent twice.

Asked CVC about a certificate program or course articulation

Fix — certificates are awarded by individual colleges, not CVC. Articulation lives at assist.org. Both questions go to your Home College's counselor, not us.

Assumed a "W" was a system bug

Fix — a "W" on transcript is the normal outcome of dropping after the no-notation deadline. It doesn't affect GPA. Different from a failure or a denial.

Step-by-step guides

Open any chapter. Each one includes the gotchas that send students to support.

Register for a class
5 steps with the prereq + payment gotchas inline
i.
Search at search.cvc.edu and click Add Class on the section you want.
ii.
Log in through your Home College (the one you already attend). Gotcha — if your Home College isn't listed, you can't cross-enroll. Apply directly to the Teaching College at icangotocollege.com instead.
iii.
Type your full name on the consent form to sign it.
iv.
Fill the enrollment form. Upload prerequisite documents if asked. Confirm CA residency. Gotcha — non-CCC prereqs (4-year, private, out-of-state) need manual review and take 5–7 business days, not 3.
v.
Click Submit, then pay immediately. Always click Confirm Payment after the bank step — this is the single biggest cause of payment tickets.
Before you submit a registration ticket
Pay tuition
The Confirm Payment trap and how to avoid it
i.
From your CVC dashboard, click Pay Fees on the course.
ii.
You'll be redirected to the Teaching College's payment page (often TouchNet, NelNet, or Cybersource). Enter your card.
iii.
After the bank approves, you're redirected back to a CVC page with a Confirm Payment button. Don't close the browser yet. If you do, your card is charged but the Teaching College won't see you as paid. They may drop you. Click Confirm Payment.
iv.
Verify the dashboard now says Enrolled & Paid. If it still says Pay Now, the confirmation didn't register — see the error decoder.
Drop or switch a class
Order matters — and money doesn't transfer

Just dropping

i.
Log in to search.cvc.edu.
ii.
Class Schedule → click Drop Course on the class.
iii.
Confirm. Don't repeat-click if the page seems slow.

Switching classes

i.
Register for the new class first. This locks the seat. If it fills before you drop the old one, you've lost both.
ii.
Pay for the new class.
iii.
Drop the old class.
iv.
Request a refund from the Teaching College of the dropped class. Find the process at cvc.edu/students/refunds. Refunds take 2–6 weeks.
Important — Money does not transfer between classes. You'll pay separately for the new class and wait for the refund from the dropped one.

Find your drop deadline

Drop deadlines are per-section, not per-term. Two classes in the same semester can have different deadlines.

  • Look at the section details on your CVC dashboard.
  • Or check the Teaching College's website — most publish a deadline-by-CRN tool.
  • Or email the Teaching College's A&R office.

Two deadlines to watch: Last Day to Drop — No Notation (clean drop, no transcript record), and Last Day to Drop with "W" (drop later, get a W on transcript).

Find your class in Canvas
Always your Home College's Canvas — not the Teaching College's
i.
Log in to your CVC dashboard.
ii.
Class Schedule → click the link below your course. This opens your Home College's Canvas.
iii.
In Canvas, click View All Courses if it's not on the dashboard.
Note — it can take 24–48 hours after registering. Some colleges only provision Canvas access closer to class start. Email support after 3 business days OR within 24 hours of class start.
Use financial aid (CCPG, FAFSA, Pell)
CVC works as reimbursement, not direct pay
Read this first — per CVC's official guidance: "clicking the request box does not mean you will receive financial aid." Submitting a financial aid request during enrollment only sends the request to your Home College for review. You are still responsible for paying the Teaching College upfront while your Home College considers your request. If you don't pay, the Teaching College drops you — even if your aid is later approved.

How payment actually works

You pay the Teaching College upfront with a card. Your Home College reimburses you according to your aid package, after the fact. Aid does not get sent ahead to the Teaching College on your behalf — it always disburses through your Home College only.

CCPG (Promise Grant)

If you qualify at your Home College, CCPG generally covers per-unit fees at the Teaching College too — but only if the match works.

SSN script — the CVC enrollment form doesn't collect SSN. For CCPG to match your award at the Teaching College, they need your SSN on file. If your CCPG isn't applying, contact the Teaching College's A&R or Financial Aid office and ask them to confirm your SSN is on record. That's the fix — not a CVC bug.

Pell Grant

Pell can't be disbursed at the Teaching College. CVC students sit in a non-Title IV "shell" program at the Teaching College. Pell still works at your Home College for classes you take there. CVC courses count toward your enrollment status for aid purposes, but the disbursement is at the Home College.

Full info: cvc.edu/students/financial-aid

Get a transcript
Your home college vs. external schools

Sending grades back to your Home College

Automatic. The Teaching College sends grades to your Home College after the term ends. Allow 4–6 weeks. If grades aren't visible after that, contact your Home College's A&R first — they often just need to refresh.

Sending transcripts to a 4-year college or employer

Request from the Teaching College that taught the course — not from your Home College, not from CVC. Most colleges use Parchment.

Important when you transfer — per CVC's official guidance, "you will still need to order transcripts from each college individually." If you took three CVC classes at three different Teaching Colleges, that's three separate transcript orders, plus one from your Home College. CVC does not consolidate them.

Details: cvc.edu/students/transcripts-students

Eligibility — special cases
High school, F-1, AB540, Adult Ed, holds

The basic requirements (all must be true)

  • You are at least 18 years old.
  • You are currently enrolled at a CCC Home College. If this is your first term at your Home College — or you were recently admitted — you must either have completed a class there, or be currently taking a class that's in progress. Acceptance alone isn't enough.
  • You have at least a 2.0 GPA at your Home College — or you have not yet established a GPA (first-term students with no completed classes).
  • You have no outstanding fees at your Home College.
  • You have an in-state (California) address on file at your Home College.

Special cases

  • High school: dual-enrollment authorization from your high school + parent before registering.
  • Adult Education: authorization from your Adult Ed program.
  • F-1 visa / international: talk to your Designated School Official (DSO) first. Federal regulations restrict concurrent enrollment.
  • AB540: enrollment works fine. For fees, file CCPG / Dream Act Application separately at your Home College.
  • Hold on Home College account: clear it first. The system blocks new registrations while a hold is active.
  • Low GPA at Home College: system off-ramps you. Resolve the GPA flag at Home College before retrying.
  • Home College not on Exchange: apply directly to the Teaching College at icangotocollege.com.

Full rules: cvc.edu/students/student-eligibility

vii.

Should I email support?

Most issues fix themselves with time or a quick check. Save email for things that genuinely need a human.

Don't email — wait or self-resolve

  • Status is "Pending" anything, less than 5 business days
  • "Prerequisite Pending Review" with non-CCC prereqs, less than 7 business days
  • Canvas course missing, less than 48 hours since registering
  • Refund hasn't arrived, less than 4 weeks
  • "Course Filled" — there's no fix, find another section
  • "Status Update Failed" on a class you already dropped (display issue)
  • Question about a certificate, articulation, or degree audit (counselor at Home College)
  • Asking about a waitlist (none exist on Exchange)
  • Tuition shows out-of-state — call the Teaching College's A&R first
  • Resubmitted while another submission is pending

Yes — email now

  • Status hasn't changed in 5+ business days
  • Class starts within 48 hours and you're still pending or failed
  • Charged but never enrolled, with bank evidence
  • "Drop Failed" persisting after one careful try
  • Out-of-state tuition was charged and you're a CA resident
  • "Application Denied" with no reason given
  • You believe an eligibility decision is wrong, with documentation
  • Drop deadline is within 72 hours and the system is broken
  • Anything time-sensitive — drop deadline, exam, financial aid disbursement

A small glossary

Common terms in the dashboard and on this page, in plain English.

Home College
Your primary CCC — where you log in from. Financial aid only disburses from your Home College (Pell, FAFSA, CCPG) — no aid transfers to a Teaching College for CVC courses. Your degree or certificate is also issued by your Home College, not by any Teaching College. Choose carefully — this is the college whose name appears on your final award.
Teaching College
The CCC offering the cross-enrollment class you're taking. Where the section actually meets.
CCCID
Your 7-character California Community Colleges ID. Same across all CCCs. Required for support tickets.
CRN
Course Reference Number — the section's unique ID at the Teaching College.
AER
Authorization to Enroll Request. The form you fill out when registering. Doesn't collect SSN.
CCPG
California College Promise Grant. Per-unit fee waiver. Apply at your Home College.
Pending Person Match
Teaching College is matching your CCCID to their internal record. Normal — wait 1–3 business days.
Validated & Registered
You're fully enrolled. The good outcome.
Drop Delete Code
A Banner SIS quirk that wipes the registration record on drop. Surfaces as "Status Update Failed" — display issue, not a real failure.
Confirm Payment
The button on the post-bank CVC page that signals payment. Skipping it = card charged, college doesn't know.
