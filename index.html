Quick clarifications

Myths students keep almost-believing

CVC charges a fee for using the Exchange.
CVC charges nothing. You pay only the Teaching College's per-unit tuition ($46/unit if in-state) plus any local fees that college sets.
Financial aid will pay the Teaching College for me.
You pay upfront. Aid disburses through your Home College only, then reimburses you. Federal aid never disburses at the TC.
My Home College's residency status applies everywhere.
Each college keeps its own record. Title 5 requires every CCC to make its own residency determination. HC fix doesn't sync to TC.
If support is closed, the deadline gets extended.
It doesn't. Document your attempt with a timestamped screenshot + email. That paper trail supports a retroactive exception, not an automatic one.
"Status Update Failed" means my drop didn't go through.
Usually a display issue. Banner colleges hit this when the drop succeeded but the dashboard doesn't refresh. Don't re-attempt.
My class will appear in the Teaching College's Canvas.
Use your Home College's Canvas. The trust relationship makes the TC course appear in your HC's Canvas dashboard. The TC's Canvas won't show it.

Class begins in 48 hours and I'm still Pending
Exact steps before you panic-email
  1. Check your CVC inbox at search.cvc.edu after login. The Teaching College may have requested a document. If they did, you have to respond before they can finish.
  2. Confirm the status hasn't changed. "Pending Person Match" and "Prerequisite Pending Review" both look stuck — but they mean the Teaching College is actively reviewing you. They are not denied.
  3. Email support with the words "URGENT — CLASS START [date]" in the subject line, plus the information from the ticket builder below. Replies on time-sensitive tickets are typically same-day.
  4. While you wait, identify a backup section at search.cvc.edu. If the original Teaching College can't move fast enough, you may need to register for a different open section.
My drop deadline is today or tomorrow
What to do if drop fails or you're stuck
  1. Try the drop one time. Don't retry repeatedly — that doesn't help, and it creates duplicate records that slow resolution.
  2. Take a screenshot of any error. The exact error text determines what kind of fix is needed.
  3. Email support immediately with subject "URGENT — DROP DEADLINE [date]" and your CCCID, course/CRN, and screenshot. Use the ticket builder to format it correctly.
  4. Email the Teaching College's Admissions & Records office in parallel. Your email is documented intent to drop before the deadline. If anything goes wrong on our end, that record protects you. Find the A&R email at cvc.edu/students/refunds.
Note. Drop deadlines are per-section, not per-term. Look up your specific date on the Teaching College's deadline tool — don't assume the standard semester schedule.
It's after-hours or a weekend and a deadline is approaching
Plan B when support is closed

CVC support staffs business hours, Monday through Friday. If you're reading this at midnight on Saturday with a Tuesday drop deadline, here's the realistic plan:

  1. Document everything right now. Screenshot the error, the timestamp, the dashboard state. Note exactly what you tried. This timestamped record is your strongest protection if something goes wrong.
  2. Email CVC support anyway. Use subject line "URGENT — DEADLINE [exact date and time]". Even though no one reads it until Monday, the timestamp on your email proves you reached out before the deadline. Use the ticket builder.
  3. Email the Teaching College's Admissions & Records office in parallel. Find their email at cvc.edu/students/refunds or on the college's website. Tell them: "I attempted to drop CRN [N] on [date/time] and the system showed [error]. I want this on record before the [date] deadline." That's documented intent at the TC, separate from CVC.
  4. Don't keep retrying. Repeated drop attempts create duplicate records that slow Monday's resolution. One careful try, screenshot, then stop.
  5. If the deadline truly passes despite your timely effort, both CVC and the Teaching College can advocate for a late-drop petition with that paper trail. The system isn't designed to punish students for our errors — but the documentation is what makes advocacy possible.
Important — the deadline will not be extended just because support is closed. Your documentation creates the case for a retroactive exception, not an automatic one. Better to have the trail than not.
My card was charged but I'm not enrolled
Almost always the missing Confirm Payment click
  1. Log in to search.cvc.edu. Look at your Class Schedule. Does it say "Pay Now" or "Enrolled & Paid"?
  2. If it says "Pay Now": the system never received your payment confirmation. The fix is on our side; we can reconcile it. Email support with your CCCID, course, payment date/time, last 4 of card, and amount.
  3. If it says "Enrolled & Paid": you're fine — the dashboard caught up. Done.
  4. Don't pay again. A second payment goes to the same place and creates a refund headache, not enrollment.
Why this happens. Students close the browser after the bank confirms the charge — but before clicking the Confirm Payment button on the page CVC redirects you to. Always click Confirm Payment.

Pending Person Match
wait
What it means
The Teaching College needs to match your CCCID to a record in their system. A normal step — not a failure.
Whose ball
The Teaching College's. CVC has handed it to them.
Realistic timeline
Typically around 1–3 business days, but varies by college. Five or more days is unusual.
When to email support
After 5 business days, or if class starts within 48 hours.
Action now — nothing. Don't resubmit; don't email yet. Use the date checker to confirm exactly when to follow up.
Prerequisite Pending Review
wait
What it means
Teaching College's A&R is reviewing your prerequisite documents. You are not denied — you are waiting.
Whose ball
The Teaching College's A&R office.
Timeline
Typically around 3–5 business days for CCC prereqs; usually a bit longer (around 5–7) for non-CCC documents (private, four-year, out-of-state). Each Teaching College sets its own pace.
When to email
After the timeline above, or if class starts within 48 hours.
Critical — do not resubmit while waiting. Duplicate submissions create conflicting records and slow the review. One application at a time.
Course Filled / Class is full
move on
What it means
The section reached capacity before your registration completed.
Common misread
Students often think their prereqs were rejected. They weren't — the system never got that far. The seat check happens first.
Waitlist?
No. The CVC Exchange has no waitlists or add codes. Once full, you can't queue.
Email support?
No — there's no fix. This is correct system behavior.
Three options — (i) find another open section at any Teaching College via search.cvc.edu, (ii) apply directly to the Teaching College outside CVC at icangotocollege.com — they may have their own waitlist, (iii) take the course in a future term.
Registration Failed (no specific message)
check detail
What it means
"Registration Failed" is a category, not a specific cause. The actual reason lives in the Application Errors field on your enrollment record.
Where to look
CVC dashboard → Class Schedule → click the failed enrollment → read the error message exactly.
Action — match the exact error text against this decoder. If it mentions "Course Filled," "prerequisite," "residency," or "hold," see the matching entry. If it's something else, copy the exact text and email support.
Drop Failed
email — once
Most likely cause
A hold on your Teaching College account. By far the most common reason — the TC's SIS refuses to drop a student with an active hold (often financial, sometimes registration). Banner-based colleges (Saddleback, College of the Mountains/CMC, others) hit this pattern frequently.
Other causes
Drop-without-W deadline already passed at the TC, OR a real system / middleware error. Each has a different fix path.
Don't retry
Repeated attempts create duplicate records that slow resolution. One careful try, then stop.
Time pressure
If your drop-without-W deadline is within 72 hours, mark the email URGENT and follow the parallel-path advice.
What works fastest — (i) check your Teaching College account for any hold (financial, registration, or other) and clear it directly with the TC's A&R office; (ii) email CVC support once with your CCCID, course/CRN, an error screenshot, and the deadline date — the team can request a manual drop on the back end if needed; (iii) email the TC's A&R in parallel for documented timely intent. Use the ticket builder.

read inbox first

Common reasons

First step
Check your CVC inbox — most colleges send a denial reason there.
If no reason given — email support so we can ask the college on your behalf. Don't keep resubmitting; that re-triggers the same denial.

Don't re-attempt
Re-dropping a class that's already dropped creates problems.

Tuition shows $400+ for 3 units
don't pay
What it means
Your California residency wasn't confirmed at the Teaching College, so the system is charging you out-of-state tuition. Each college keeps its own residency record — your Home College's record doesn't automatically transfer.
In-state rate
$46 per unit. $138 for 3 units. $184 for 4. Real ticket examples we've seen of out-of-state pricing: $1,000 (3 units), $1,400 (3 units), $1,864 (4 units). If your number is anywhere near 3–9× the in-state rate, it's a residency issue — not a bug.
The 2-step fix
(1) Confirm your residency is up to date at your Home College's A&R office. (2) Send proof of that residency status (a screenshot of your HC portal showing "California Resident," or a letter from HC's A&R) to the Teaching College's A&R office. The TC must update their own residency flag — neither CVC nor your HC can do it for them.
Special cases
AB540 — file with the TC separately (not just HC). Active-duty military — your HC's Veterans Resource Center sends a Parent School Authorization Letter to the TC. Recently reclassified — see the full residency guide below.
Do not pay until tuition recalculates. If you've already paid the out-of-state rate and your residency is later corrected, the Teaching College decides whether to rebill or refund — CVC can't issue refunds. Read the full §vi residency guide for the email templates and special-population paths.
My Home College fixed my residency but the Teaching College still charges me out-of-state
two-college fix
Why this happens
Residency lives in each college's own SIS — there is no single statewide residency record. When your HC reclassifies you, that update stays at your HC unless you also push it to the TC. CVC reads what the TC has on file at the moment you registered, which is why your tuition was set then.
If you haven't paid yet
Email the Teaching College's A&R office. Subject: "Residency reclassification — please update for CVC enrollment." Include your TC student ID, the course name, the exact rate you're being charged, and proof of your CA residency at your HC (screenshot or HC A&R letter). The TC updates their record; tuition recalculates. Then email CVC support to confirm the batch application reflects the new status before you pay.
If you've already paid the out-of-state rate
CVC can't update the batch application's residency flag once payment has cleared — that's a designed limit. Whether you get a refund or rebill is the Teaching College's decision: contact their bursar/student accounts office and request a residency reclassification refund. Allow 4–6 weeks. Some TCs rebill automatically; others require a written request.
What CVC can do — verify the data flow, confirm what the TC has on file, and (pre-payment only) update the batch application's residency flag once the TC confirms. What CVC can't do — override the TC's residency determination, issue refunds, or change a paid batch application. See §vi residency guide for the full process.

Don't pay twice
A second payment goes to the same place. It doesn't fix the first; it just creates a refund problem.

Reconciliations are typically resolved within a couple of business days, sometimes longer depending on the college.
Email once — with CCCID, course/CRN, payment date and time, last 4 of card, and amount charged. Use the ticket builder. We do the rest.

"System can't verify my community college account" — wrong email at signup
3 accounts, align them
What this looks like
You're trying to set up CVC for the first time, you entered a personal email (Gmail, Yahoo, iCloud) on your profile, and CVC says it can't verify your community college enrollment. Or your school issued you a .edu address but you signed up at OpenCCC with a non-edu address and now the SSO bridge isn't connecting the two records.
First — are you actually enrolled at a CCC?
CVC isn't a college you apply to. It routes between CCCs you're already enrolled at. If you've never applied to a community college, apply first via icangotocollege.com. Once you're admitted and registered for at least one class at your Home College, return to CVC. See the first-time setup guide for the full picture.
If you ARE enrolled but verification fails
The fix is almost always at OpenCCC, not CVC. Go to opencccapply.net → My Account → Personal Information. Update your email to match what your Home College has on file (typically your school's .edu address, but check your HC's portal to confirm). Save. Allow 24–48 hours to resync; CVC pulls from OpenCCC at every login, so the next attempt should succeed.
Why the email matters
Many HCs use single sign-on (SSO) for CVC, which authenticates against your school-issued email. When the email on OpenCCC and the email at your HC don't match, the SSO bridge can't link the two records, and CVC has nothing to verify against. The system then reports "no community college enrollment found" — even though you ARE enrolled. The mismatch is the bug, not your enrollment.
Edge cases
Multiple OpenCCC accounts — if you've created OpenCCC profiles under different emails over the years, the system has multiple CCCIDs for you and can't pick which is real. See duplicate-login. Just admitted, no class yet — admission alone isn't enough; you need to be currently enrolled in (or have completed) a class. HC isn't on CVC — not every CCC is live. Check the colleges list.
Don't keep retrying. Repeated sign-in attempts with the wrong email don't help and can trigger temporary account locks. Update OpenCCC, wait 24–48 hours, then retry. If still failing, email CVC support with your CCCID, name, HC, and the exact error text — we can verify what the SSO bridge is seeing.
"Invalid CCCID" / "CCCID not found" at registration
SSO config issue
What it means
Your Home College's login system isn't passing your CCCID through to CVC during single-sign-on. Without the CCCID, the Teaching College has no way to identify you and the registration is rejected.
Why it happens
Three common causes: (i) you have a CCCID at OpenCCC but your Home College's IT didn't link it in their system; (ii) you have multiple OpenCCC accounts under different emails — the system can't tell which is "you"; (iii) you're a brand-new student whose CCCID hasn't migrated to the HC's records yet.
First step (5 minutes)
Go to openccc.net, sign in or recover your account, and confirm your CCCID. Verify it matches what your Home College shows on your student portal.
If they don't match
Contact your Home College's A&R office (not CVC, not Tech Center) and ask them to update your CCCID in their student record. CVC can't update HC records.
Real talk — this is the #1 root cause of unsolvable cross-enrollment failures. There's no streamlined fix from the CVC side; it requires the HC to align your record. If your HC isn't responsive, an alternative is to apply directly to the Teaching College via icangotocollege.com while the HC sorts itself out.
I selected the wrong Home College at signup
drop + re-enroll
What this is
When you first cross-enrolled, you picked a Home College where you have a login (maybe an old account) but it's not where you currently attend. The cross-enrollment goes through, but downstream things break: financial aid emails go to the wrong place, transcripts may route to the wrong institution, MIS reporting at the TC may be off.
Can it be changed?
No — manually changing the Home College after the fact is not supported. The system's verification and authentication paths weren't designed for that, and a back-end change creates more problems than it solves.
The fix
Drop the cross-enrolled course and re-enroll, this time selecting the correct Home College. Yes, you'll need to pay again and wait for a refund on the original. Yes, this is annoying. There's no shortcut.
Already finished the class?
Email CVC support — late-stage HC corrections are rare and case-by-case. They may need to escalate internally.
Don't keep cross-enrolling under the wrong HC hoping it sorts itself out. It won't. The longer the wrong HC is on your record, the messier downstream gets.
"Add Class" appears but registration fails — and it's evening
wait until midnight PST
What it means
There's a quirk in the timing of when the registration window opens. The "Add Class" button appears at 4 PM PST the day before open enrollment — but registrations submitted between 4 PM PST and midnight PST will silently fail. The system is technically open at midnight in the system's clock, not at 4 PM in yours.
When this hits you
You're trying to register the evening before classes open at your Teaching College. You see "Add Class," click it, fill out the form — and get a generic error or stuck state.
The fix
Wait until after midnight Pacific Time on the open-enrollment date and try again. Don't keep retrying in the meantime — the failed attempts can leave artifacts that complicate the next try.
When to email support
If you tried after midnight PST and it still fails. Otherwise this resolves itself in a few hours.
Tell your friends — this gotcha is real and unfixed. If you know the open date, register Day 1 morning, not Day 0 evening.
Co-requisite lab error: "prerequisite not met"
contact TC A&R
What it means
You're enrolled in the lecture (e.g., ANTH 101), but the system blocks the companion lab (ANTH 101L) saying you don't meet the prerequisite — even though the lab is a co-requisite, not a prerequisite. The check fires before recognizing your concurrent enrollment.
Why it happens
The Teaching College's SIS evaluates the lab as a standalone course, not as paired with the lecture. The instructor and TC A&R can manually clear it.
First step
Email the lab instructor explaining you're already in the lecture. Loop in the Teaching College's A&R office. They typically clear the override within 1–2 business days.
CVC support's role
Limited — this is a TC-side override. CVC can escalate but the actual unlock happens at the TC.
Action — email the TC instructor + A&R first. CVC support second if no response within 2 business days.
Login error: "duplicate account" or password reset loop
try logout + cookies
What it means
You have multiple OpenCCC profiles (often from old applications under different emails or schools), and the SSO sees them as duplicates — blocks login or sends you in a password-reset loop.
Try this first (90 seconds)
(i) Log out of all OpenCCC sessions globally — there's a "log out everywhere" link on OpenCCC.
(ii) Clear browser cookies for openccc.net and your CCC's SSO domain.
(iii) Try a different browser, or incognito/private mode.
(iv) Confirm your CCCID is correctly linked at your Home College's portal.
If still broken
This is a CCC Tech Center issue, not a CVC issue. Email cccsupport@openccc.net with: your full name, both emails involved (if known), date of birth, and a screenshot. They can merge or retire the duplicate profile.
Why CVC can't fix it
CVC doesn't have admin access to OpenCCC profiles. The CCC Tech Center owns the identity layer.
Don't email CVC first — try the 4-step browser fix, then go to CCC Tech Center if it persists. CVC routes you to them anyway.
Transcript on hold for "Records Research"
verify name spelling
What it means
Parchment received your transcript request but couldn't match it to a record at the Teaching College. Almost always a name mismatch — the name you used when ordering doesn't exactly match what the TC has on file.
Common cases
Last name with two parts (e.g., "Pereyra Palacio" vs "Pereyra"); preferred vs legal name; hyphenated names; accents/diacritics omitted; first/middle initial added or dropped.
First step
Look up exactly what name the Teaching College has on file (usually visible in their student portal or on a prior transcript). Re-submit the Parchment order with that exact name spelling.
If still stuck
Email Parchment's CVC support (cvc.customersupport@parchment.com) with your order confirmation number, the email used, your CCCID, and the exact name as it appears at the Teaching College.
A past payment was declined and I just found out
documentation request
What this is
A scenario that surfaces months later: the Teaching College contacts you to say they didn't receive payment, and CVC's records show the original payment was declined — but you weren't notified at the time. Rare but real.
What CVC can provide
Documentation of the declined transaction: timestamp, last-4 of the card, amount. Useful for verifying with your bank and for re-submitting payment if needed.
Email what you have
CCCID, the term/course in question, what the Teaching College told you, and the approximate date of the original attempt. CVC can pull the transaction record from their side.
Realistic outcome
If the decline is documented, you can resubmit the payment — sometimes with help from the TC if the term is closed.
Email support with your transaction details — they'll provide documentation you can use with the TC and your bank.
"Live Seat Count: Unavailable" — can't tell if seats are open
try anyway
What it means
CVC's seat-count display can fall back to "Unavailable" when the Teaching College's SIS isn't returning live seat data — usually a temporary integration issue, not a sold-out class. Has been seen as a cluster at some TCs (Coalinga, Lemoore, Peralta-area colleges) tied to specific SIS configurations.
What to do
Try the registration anyway — the seat check happens server-side at submit, so an "Unavailable" display doesn't actually block the attempt. If your AER goes through and reaches "Pending Validation," seats were available. If it fails with "Course Filled," they weren't.
If it fails repeatedly
Email CVC support with CCCID, course, TC, exact timestamp, and the unavailable display. We verify with the TC's A&R whether seats actually exist and report any cluster patterns to the implementation tracker.
Don't wait. The display issue doesn't gate registration. Each minute you wait, real seats may be claimed by someone else who tried.
My prereq application was denied even though I qualify
re-review path
First — read the denial reason
Check your CVC inbox for the denial email. It usually states one of: missing documentation, insufficient prereq evidence, class already started, or system error during waiver. Different reasons need different fixes.
If "missing documentation" or "insufficient prereq"
Re-submit with stronger evidence: official transcript (not unofficial), a letter from the prereq instructor confirming you've completed the equivalent, or your HC's assessment results. Email these directly to the Teaching College's A&R office along with your re-review request.
If "class already started"
CVC stops accepting registrations after the TC's add deadline (typically the day classes begin). If your AER was submitted before but the prereq review took past the start date, the TC may still accept a late add — you'll need to apply directly to the TC and use the instructor's add code.
If "system error" — prereq waiver wasn't applied
This is a CVC bug worth reporting. Email CVC support with: CCCID, course, X-tracing ID (in your CVC inbox), and the exact denial text. We can re-trigger the prereq waiver and re-submit your AER.

First time using CVC? Here's what each account does
Three accounts work together — get them aligned, or verification fails
If verification is failing right now — see "System can't verify my community college account". This guide is the foundational explainer.

The most common confusion at first sign-up is thinking CVC Exchange is itself a college you apply to. It's not. CVC routes you between CCCs you're already enrolled at, which means three separate accounts have to line up before CVC can verify you. When a student tells us "the system can't find my college account," it's almost always because one of these three is missing or out of sync with the others.

The three accounts

  1. OpenCCC account — your statewide California Community Colleges identity. This is where your CCCID (the 7-character ID like CAF3028) lives. One per person, ever. If you've ever applied to any CCC, you have one. Found at opencccapply.net.
  2. Home College student account — your record at the CCC you primarily attend. This is where you log in to your HC's portal, where your transcripts live, where your degree is issued, where financial aid disburses. Created when you applied via CCCApply and were admitted to that college. Your HC almost always assigns you a school-issued email (typically a .edu address) when you enroll.
  3. CVC Exchange profile — your account on cvc.edu. It doesn't exist independently — it pulls from OpenCCC at every login and looks up your enrollment at the HC. If your OpenCCC record doesn't match your HC's record, CVC has nothing to verify against and shows you as not enrolled.

Why the three have to align

CVC's verification step asks: "Does this OpenCCC profile (with this CCCID) have an active enrollment at one of our partner CCCs?" To answer "yes," the data has to be:

  • Same person — your name, DOB, and SSN (last 4) on OpenCCC must match the same fields on your HC student record.
  • Same CCCID — your HC ties their internal student record to your CCCID. Common breakage: students create a second OpenCCC account under a different email (different CCCID) and try to log in with that one.
  • Active enrollment — admission alone isn't enough. You have to be currently enrolled in a class at your HC (or have completed one).

Email mismatches — the most common cause

Your OpenCCC email and your HC student email can be different — that's allowed. But:

  • Many HCs use SSO (single sign-on) for CVC login, which authenticates against your school-issued .edu email. If you signed up at OpenCCC with your personal email (Gmail, Yahoo) and try to log into CVC via your HC's SSO, the system may not bridge the two records correctly.
  • If you used a personal (non-edu) email at OpenCCC, CVC may default to "I can't verify your community college enrollment" because the SSO bridge from your HC didn't recognize the email.
  • Fix: update your OpenCCC email to match what your HC has on file (typically your school's .edu address). Update at opencccapply.net → My Account → Personal Information. Allow 24–48 hours for CVC to resync.

If you don't know what email your HC has on file, log into your HC's student portal — your profile page typically shows the email of record. Or contact your HC's A&R office.

If you've never applied to a CCC before

You can't use CVC Exchange. Apply to a community college first via icangotocollege.com (the CCCApply portal). Once admitted and enrolled in at least one class, your HC's record will be in the system — then return to CVC and your verification will succeed.

Quick checklist before you panic

  1. Are you currently enrolled in a class at a California Community College? (Admission alone isn't enough.)
  2. Is the email on your OpenCCC profile the same one your HC has? (If unsure: HC portal → Profile.)
  3. Do you have only one OpenCCC account? (Multiple = multiple CCCIDs = login chaos.)
  4. Is your HC active on CVC Exchange (not just listed)? See the colleges list on cvc.edu.

If all four are yes and CVC still can't verify, see err-verify-account for the diagnostic and fix path.

What is the CVC Exchange? (60-second primer)
Read this if you're new — orientation to home/teaching college, cost, journey

The California Virtual Campus (CVC) Exchange is a system run by the California Community Colleges that lets you take a class at a different CCC than the one you primarily attend — without a separate application. Think of it as a routing layer between 115 community colleges.

The two-college concept

  • Home College — the CCC you primarily attend. Where you log in from. Where your degree or certificate is issued. Where all financial aid disburses (Pell, FAFSA, CCPG). The college whose name appears on your final award.
  • Teaching College — the CCC offering the specific class you want. Where the class meets. Where you pay for that class. Where the transcript of that class lives until you order it sent.

What CVC handles vs what your colleges handle

  • CVC Exchange handles: routing your registration, payment processing, the data passing between colleges.
  • Your Home College handles: aid disbursement, your degree/certificate, your overall record, counseling and articulation.
  • Your Teaching College handles: the actual class, grading, the transcript of that class, drop deadlines, refunds.

What it costs

In-state CCC tuition is $46/unit for California residents. A 3-unit class is $138; a 4-unit class is $184. Out-of-state students pay much more. If your tuition shows $400+ for 3 units, your residency wasn't confirmed at the Teaching College — see the tuition entry.

Who's eligible

Concurrently enrolled at a CCC (Home College), 18 years old, 2.0 GPA (or no established GPA), no outstanding fees, and a California address on file. Special populations (high school, F-1, AB540, Adult Ed) need extra paperwork — see §vi eligibility.

Eligibility — special cases
High school, F-1, AB540, Adult Ed, holds
For any planning question — talk to your Home College's academic counselor first. They know your degree path, prerequisites, transfer plan, and special-admit status better than any FAQ can. The list below covers eligibility rules; whether a specific class fits your plan is counselor territory.

The basic requirements (all must be true)

  • You are at least 18 years old.
  • You are currently enrolled at a CCC Home College. If this is your first term at your Home College — or you were recently admitted — you must either have completed a class there, or be currently taking a class that's in progress. Acceptance alone isn't enough.
  • You have at least a 2.0 GPA at your Home College — or you have not yet established a GPA (first-term students with no completed classes).
  • You have no outstanding fees at your Home College.
  • You have an in-state (California) address on file at your Home College.

Populations NOT eligible to cross-enroll via CVC

  • Dual-enrolled high school students — cvc.edu states explicitly: "Dual-enrolled high school students are not eligible to enroll in courses via the CVC Exchange, due to the unique policies and procedures in place." If you're an HS dual-enrollment student, talk to your Home College's special-admit counselor or apply directly to the Teaching College.
  • International students on F-1 visa — federal visa regulations restrict concurrent enrollment outside your sponsoring school. Per cvc.edu: F-1 students are "not able to enroll in courses via the CVC Exchange." Talk to your Designated School Official (DSO) about your options.
  • Adult Education students — typically not permitted via the Exchange. Contact your Adult Ed program coordinator.

Special cases that ARE eligible (with extra steps)

  • AB540 (California Nonresident Tuition Exemption): Eligible. First establish AB540 status with your Home College. For CCPG, apply at the Teaching College through CCCApply or the California Dream Act Application with the TC's CADAA code.
  • Veterans: Eligible. Your Home College's Veterans Resource Center handles benefit certification and contacts the Teaching College with a Parent School Authorization Letter. Talk to your HC's VRC before registering.
  • Returning adult / non-traditional students: Eligible if you meet the basic requirements above (CCC enrollment, 18+, 2.0 GPA or none yet, no fees, CA address).

Other things that block enrollment

  • Hold on Home College account: clear it first. The system blocks new registrations while a hold is active.
  • Low GPA at Home College: system off-ramps you. Resolve the GPA flag at HC before retrying.
  • Home College not on Exchange: apply directly to the Teaching College at icangotocollege.com.

Fix California residency for CVC tuition
Why your HC's "fix" doesn't sync to the Teaching College — and how to actually correct it
Talk to both colleges' A&R offices first. Residency determinations are made by each college separately under California Title 5 — CVC support can verify the data flow but cannot override either college's determination. The advice below covers the process; whether a specific document satisfies a specific college's residency rules is up to their A&R staff.

This is one of the top reasons students email support. The frustration is real: you're a California resident, you've lived here for years, your Home College already has you classified as in-state — and yet your CVC tuition shows out-of-state pricing of $1,000+ for a single class. The reason is structural, not a bug, and the fix is a specific 2-step process.

Why each college has its own residency record

Under California Education Code, every community college is required to make its own residency determination for every student it enrolls — including cross-enrollment students. There is no shared statewide residency database. Your Home College's "California Resident" classification lives in their SIS (Banner, Colleague, or PeopleSoft); the Teaching College's classification lives in their SIS. CVC reads what the TC has on file at the moment you submit the AER form, and that becomes the basis for tuition.

So when your residency status is wrong on a CVC course, the question isn't "did my HC fix it" — it's "did the Teaching College update their record."

The 2-step fix (most students)

  1. Confirm your residency at your Home College. Log into your HC's portal and verify your residency classification reads "California Resident" (or your HC's equivalent label). If it doesn't, contact your HC's A&R office with proof: utility bill, CA driver's license, lease/mortgage, voter registration, CA tax return — your HC can tell you which documents they need.
  2. Push that classification to the Teaching College. Email the TC's A&R office with: (a) your TC student ID, (b) the CVC course you're enrolled in, (c) the rate you're being charged, and (d) proof of HC residency — a screenshot of your HC portal showing the CA Resident status, or a letter from your HC A&R. Ask them to update your residency classification in their system. Their A&R is who fixes it — not their financial aid office, not CVC, not your HC.

Once the TC updates their record, your tuition recalculates to the in-state rate. Email CVC support if you want us to verify the batch application reflects the correct status before you pay.

Special case — AB540 (California Nonresident Tuition Exemption)

AB540 grants in-state tuition rates to certain undocumented and other qualifying students who attended a CA high school and meet specific criteria. AB540 status is determined by each college separately, just like regular residency. Process:

  1. File the California Nonresident Tuition Exemption Request (CNTER) form at your Home College. They review and approve.
  2. File the same CNTER form again at the Teaching College. Their A&R reviews and approves separately. Some TCs accept a copy of the HC's approval letter as part of the documentation; some require fresh documentation.
  3. Once the TC approves AB540, your CVC tuition recalculates to in-state.

For fee waiver coverage: apply for CCPG via California Dream Act Application (CADAA) using the Teaching College's CADAA school code. CCPG funding flows through whichever college you applied at.

Special case — Active-duty military

Active-duty service members stationed in California (and their dependents) qualify for in-state tuition under California Education Code §68075 regardless of formal residency. Process:

  1. Provide your military orders or LES (Leave and Earnings Statement) to your Home College's Veterans Resource Center (VRC). They certify your military status.
  2. The HC's VRC sends a Parent School Authorization Letter to the Teaching College with confirmation of your in-state military status. (You may need to request the PSA explicitly — call the HC's VRC and ask for it by name.)
  3. The TC processes the PSA and updates their residency flag to in-state for tuition purposes.

Don't wait until after registration — talk to your HC's VRC before you register through CVC so the PSA is in place when the AER form is submitted.

Special case — Veterans benefits (post-service)

If you're using VA benefits (GI Bill, etc.) for a CVC course, your HC's VRC handles benefit certification and sends a Parent School Authorization Letter to the Teaching College. The benefits flow through your HC; the PSA tells the TC you're cleared. Talk to your HC's VRC before registering.

Special case — recently reclassified (HC fixed it after you paid)

If you registered while your HC had you classified as non-resident, paid the out-of-state tuition, then later got reclassified to CA resident — refunding the overpayment is the Teaching College's call, not CVC's. Some Teaching Colleges:

  • Rebill automatically when their A&R updates the residency flag retroactively (favorable but not universal).
  • Require a written request to the bursar/student accounts office — usually a one-page form or email referencing the term, course, and updated residency status.
  • Decline retroactive refunds citing a "frozen at registration" policy. Rare but it happens.

Allow 4–6 weeks for any TC-side refund process. CVC's batch application can't be retroactively updated once payment has cleared — that's a designed limit (see §ix lim-residency).

Special case — you're genuinely an out-of-state resident

If you live outside California and are charged the non-resident rate, the system is working as designed. CVC Exchange does not waive non-resident tuition for purely out-of-state students. Possible paths if applicable: (a) AB540 if you meet the high-school-attendance criteria, (b) military exemption if active duty, (c) wait until you've established California residency through your HC (typically a year of physical presence + intent — your HC's A&R will tell you what counts).

What CVC support can and can't do

Can: verify what the Teaching College has on file, confirm what your batch application captured at registration, update the batch application's residency flag pre-payment once the TC confirms the change, route you to the right office.

Can't: override the Teaching College's residency determination, change a residency flag after payment has cleared, issue refunds (refunds always come from the Teaching College's bursar, not CVC), influence Title 5 residency policy.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Paying first, fixing later. Once you've paid the non-resident rate, you're at the TC's discretion for a refund. Always verify the rate is correct before clicking Pay.
  • Assuming the HC fix syncs to the TC. It doesn't. Each college maintains its own record. The 2-step fix is required.
  • Asking your HC to update the TC's record. Your HC has no access to the TC's SIS. Only the TC's A&R can change the TC's residency flag.
  • Asking CVC to issue a refund. CVC routes the data; refunds and rebills are the Teaching College's bursar.

Get a 1098-T tax form for a CVC class
Each Teaching College issues its own — CVC and your HC don't have it

The IRS Form 1098-T is the tuition statement colleges send to students who paid qualifying education expenses. For a CVC course, the Teaching College that taught the class is the college that issues the 1098-T — not CVC, not your Home College. This trips students up every January when they go looking for the form on their HC's portal and don't see it.

Where to find it

  1. Identify which Teaching College taught the class. Your CVC dashboard's Class Schedule shows the TC for each course you've taken.
  2. Contact that TC's Bursar / Student Accounts office. Tell them you took a CVC Exchange class with them in [year] and need a 1098-T. They'll either point you to a self-service portal, mail it, or email it.
  3. Repeat for every TC you used. If you took CVC classes at three different Teaching Colleges, you'll receive three separate 1098-Ts. CVC does not consolidate them.
Common confusion — students assume their Home College's 1098-T includes the CVC class because that's where their financial aid disbursed. It doesn't. The TC issues a 1098-T because the TC received your tuition payment. Each TC = each form.

If you can't reach the TC's portal

You may not have a TC student login (CVC doesn't normally provision one). Email the TC's Bursar / Student Accounts office directly with: your full name, your CCCID, the term and course you took, and the email and mailing address you want the form sent to. Most TCs accommodate this.

Note: this entry is informational only. For tax advice, talk to a tax professional — CVC support, your HC, and your TC's bursar can confirm what they sent and where, but none of us can advise on whether a specific expense is deductible.

When does the summer / fall / spring schedule appear?
Each Teaching College sets its own publish date — and the registration window is separate

"When can I register?" is one of the most common pre-registration questions. The answer is more nuanced than a single date — there are two distinct events, set independently by each Teaching College.

Two different dates to track

  • Schedule visibility — when the TC publishes their term catalog, making courses searchable on cvc.edu. Typically around 60–90 days before the term starts, but each TC sets its own date. Some publish all at once; some roll out by department.
  • Registration window opens — when the "Add Class" button activates for cross-enrollment. Often the same day as schedule visibility, but not always — some TCs publish the catalog first as a preview, then open registration a week or two later.

Typical CCC term cadence (varies)

  • Summer term — schedules typically appear March–April; registration opens shortly after.
  • Fall term — schedules typically appear April–May; registration opens by June for most TCs.
  • Spring term — schedules typically appear October–November; registration opens by December.

These windows are typical but not guaranteed. Each TC publishes on their own academic calendar — check the specific TC's website for confirmed dates.

The 4 PM PST gotcha

Even when the TC announces a registration open date, CVC's "Add Class" button appears at 4 PM PST the day before. But registrations submitted between 4 PM and midnight PST will silently fail. Wait until midnight Pacific Time on the announced open date. Full err-window details.

Pre-registration checklist

While you wait for visibility, make sure your account is ready:

  • Confirmed enrolled at your Home College for the upcoming term (or actively taking a class if first-term).
  • No outstanding holds at your HC.
  • Residency confirmed at HC (so the AER form pulls correct data).
  • If you're a special population (AB540, military, veterans), paperwork already filed at HC.
Don't email asking when courses will be visible. CVC support can't accelerate a TC's catalog publish. Bookmark cvc.edu and check periodically; most browsers can set a watch on a specific search URL.
Find your past CVC enrollments (and their transcripts)
Especially if you took CCC Online classes years ago and need them now

This comes up most often when students apply to graduate programs (PA school, nursing, transfer) and need transcripts from every college they've ever taken classes at — including CVC Exchange courses they may not remember the details of. The good news: every record exists. The not-so-good: it's in pieces.

Where your past CVC records live

  • Your CVC Exchange dashboard at search.cvc.edu shows your enrollment history within the current Exchange platform — typically going back to the launch of CVC's current system.
  • Each Teaching College keeps the academic record (grades, transcripts) for the class you took with them. They issue the official transcript, not CVC.
  • Older "CCC Online" enrollments (the predecessor to CVC Exchange) may not appear in the current dashboard. Those records are at the Teaching Colleges that hosted the courses — but you may need to remember which colleges those were.

If your dashboard is missing past enrollments

  1. Check your Home College's transcript first. Per CVC's official process, classes you took via Exchange were automatically transcripted back to your HC (typically within 3 weeks of term end). Your HC transcript should list every CVC class as transfer credit, including the Teaching College and term.
  2. Check your Canvas history. Log into your HC's Canvas and click "All Courses." Past CVC courses should appear in the archive — even if just as a course shell. The course code and term will tell you which TC.
  3. Email CVC support with: your CCCID, your name on file, approximate years/terms you took CVC courses. We can look up your historical batch applications and tell you which TCs you used.
  4. Then contact each TC's A&R to order an official transcript. (CVC does not order transcripts on your behalf — see §vi transcripts.)
If you applied for older CCC Online classes via FERPA-protected paper records — note that some CCCs purge inactive student records after a fixed retention period (often 5 years). If a TC's A&R can't find your record, ask if they have a long-term archive or if it was purged.
Change the email on your CVC account
CVC syncs from OpenCCC — update there, not on CVC directly

Your CVC Exchange account doesn't have its own editable email field — it pulls from your OpenCCC profile. Updating the email on CVC alone won't stick; the next sync overwrites it. The fix is to change it at OpenCCC.

Steps

  1. Go to opencccapply.net and sign in with your existing OpenCCC credentials.
  2. Open My Account → Personal Information. Update the email address. Save.
  3. Allow 24–48 hours for the change to propagate to CVC. The next time you log into search.cvc.edu, your new email should reflect.
  4. If your OpenCCC email is what you sign in with (not just contact), update your sign-in email at OpenCCC's account settings — that's a separate field.
Edge case — if you can't sign in to OpenCCC because you've lost access to the email tied to it, contact OpenCCC support directly (not CVC). They handle account recovery.

If your Home College changed email systems on you

Some HCs migrate from one email provider to another (e.g., from Google Workspace to Microsoft) and can break login flows for ex-students. If the change locked you out, contact your HC's IT helpdesk first — they can issue a new credential. Then update OpenCCC and CVC will resync.

My class was cancelled by the Teaching College
Refunds come from the TC's bursar, not CVC

Sometimes a Teaching College cancels a section before it starts (low enrollment, instructor unavailability). Your CVC dashboard may or may not catch up immediately. The refund process runs through the TC, not CVC.

Steps

  1. Confirm the cancellation with the Teaching College's A&R office. Get the cancellation in writing (email or formal notice) — you'll need it for the refund.
  2. Contact the TC's Bursar / Student Accounts office with that confirmation. State that you paid through CVC for [course] in [term] and the section was cancelled. Ask for a refund processed back to your original payment method. Allow 4–6 weeks.
  3. Email CVC support in parallel — we can update your batch application's status to "cancelled" so the dashboard reflects reality. We can't issue the refund (only the TC can), but we can confirm to the TC that you paid through us.
  4. If you used financial aid at the TC (CCPG, etc.), that part of the refund may flow back to the aid program rather than to you. The TC's financial aid office handles that side.
Different from a self-drop refund — when YOU drop a class within the refund period, that's a different process. See §vi residency for non-resident refund issues, or the cvc.edu refunds page for general drop refunds. Cancelled-by-TC is its own path.
I paid the Teaching College directly (outside CVC's payment flow)
Possible to reconcile, but the dashboard won't auto-update

Sometimes CVC's payment page fails repeatedly (TouchNet glitches, Cybersource timeouts, the TC's processor down) and the only way to actually pay is to go directly to the Teaching College's portal or call their cashier. This works to keep your enrollment alive — but CVC's dashboard won't know about the payment until someone tells it.

If you've already paid the TC directly

  1. Email CVC support with: CCCID, course, term, the date and method of your direct payment, and the receipt/reference number from the TC.
  2. We verify with the Teaching College's bursar that the payment cleared. Once confirmed, we update your batch application's payment status — your dashboard will reflect "Enrolled & Paid" within 1–2 business days.
  3. You don't need to do anything more at the TC's end once payment is confirmed.

If you're considering paying directly — pause first

Direct payment is a workaround, not a recommended path. Try once more through CVC's flow (different browser, different card, after-hours retry) before going around it. Direct payment can create reconciliation issues if you miss a step. If CVC's payment page repeatedly fails:

  • Email CVC support describing the exact error message you see.
  • Ask if the TC's payment processor is having a known outage (TouchNet has had multi-hour outages).
  • Wait an hour and retry through CVC.

Only go direct if the deadline is imminent and CVC's flow keeps failing.

Repeat a course (e.g., for nursing recency requirement)
Petition path — your Home College's counselor leads, CVC routes

Some programs (nursing being the most common) have a "recency requirement" — a course taken more than 5 or 7 years ago doesn't count toward admission. If you've already taken the equivalent course at a CCC and need to retake it, the standard "no repeat for credit" rule needs a petition.

Steps

  1. Talk to your Home College's academic counselor first. They handle academic petitions and know your degree path. They'll review whether the recency rule actually requires a repeat or if there's another way (challenge exam, recent equivalent).
  2. If repeat is the right path, your HC counselor files a course-repeat petition with HC's Articulation/Academic Standards office. Get an approval letter showing the petition is granted.
  3. Find a Teaching College offering the same C-ID equivalent course via CVC search. The course at the TC must articulate to the same C-ID as your HC course for the petition to apply.
  4. Cross-enroll through CVC normally. Upload the HC's repeat petition approval as part of your prerequisite documentation when prompted, or email it to the TC's A&R office immediately after submitting the AER.
Counselor-led decision — recency rules vary by university and program. Your counselor is the one who knows whether the schools you're applying to (UC, CSU, private) accept repeats and under what conditions. CVC routes the cross-enrollment but cannot determine whether a repeat satisfies your specific transfer plan.

Should I email support?

Most issues fix themselves with time or a quick check. Save email for things that genuinely need a human.

Don't email — wait or self-resolve

  • Status is "Pending" anything, less than 5 business days
  • "Prerequisite Pending Review" with non-CCC prereqs, less than 7 business days
  • Canvas course missing, less than 48 hours since registering
  • Refund hasn't arrived, less than 4 weeks
  • "Course Filled" — there's no fix, find another section
  • "Status Update Failed" on a class you already dropped (display issue)
  • Question about a certificate, articulation, or degree audit (counselor at Home College)
  • Asking about a waitlist (none exist on Exchange)
  • Tuition shows out-of-state — call the Teaching College's A&R first
  • Resubmitted while another submission is pending

Yes — email now

  • Status hasn't changed in 5+ business days
  • Class starts within 48 hours and you're still pending or failed
  • Charged but never enrolled, with bank evidence
  • "Drop Failed" persisting after one careful try
  • Out-of-state tuition was charged and you're a CA resident
  • "Application Denied" with no reason given
  • You believe an eligibility decision is wrong, with documentation
  • Drop deadline is within 72 hours and the system is broken
  • Anything time-sensitive — drop deadline, exam, financial aid disbursement
Final checkpoint before you email
What to expect after you send.

A realistic look at the response side, so you know what's normal and what's not.

Who actually replies — when you email cvc.customersupport@parchment.com, the first reply comes from Parchment's support team (you'll see names like Mikayla, Courtney, etc.) — they front the email queue and resolve most issues directly. For things that need back-end action (manual drops, transcript holds, payment reconciliation, system bugs), they escalate to CVC staff (Donna, Logan, others). You may see replies from both — same case, different roles.

What support CAN do

  • Investigate system errors and route to engineering for real bugs
  • Escalate stuck states to the Teaching College on your behalf
  • Reconcile "paid but not enrolled" payment records (1–2 business days)
  • Advocate for late-drop petitions with documented timely intent
  • Confirm what the Teaching College actually shows on their side
  • Provide written confirmation of system errors to support your case

What support CAN'T do

  • Override Teaching College drop deadlines or business rules
  • Disburse financial aid — that's always your Home College
  • Fix Canvas login issues — that's college IT or CCC Tech Center
  • Register you in a section that's already full (no waitlists exist)
  • Add you to a class after add codes are required
  • Issue a degree, certificate, or transcript

Realistic timing

  • First reply: 1 business day for non-urgent; same-day for time-sensitive tickets marked URGENT.
  • Investigation depth: most issues resolve in 1–2 business days. System errors needing engineering can take 2–5.
  • If you don't hear back within the SLA: reply to your existing ticket with "still waiting." Don't open a new one — it fragments the trail.
  • Weekends count against you: a Friday afternoon ticket may not be touched until Monday. If a deadline falls over the weekend, see §i. after-hours guidance.

A small glossary

Common terms in the dashboard and on this page, in plain English.

Home College
Per CVC's official definition: "the college where the student is currently enrolled at the time of registration. This is also the college where the student sets up an Educational Plan and receives benefits, including financial aid, counseling, VETS and EOPS support." Financial aid disburses from here only (Pell, FAFSA, CCPG) — no aid transfers to a Teaching College. Your degree or certificate is also issued by your Home College, not by any Teaching College. Choose carefully — this is the college whose name appears on your final award.
Teaching College
The CCC offering the cross-enrollment class you're taking. Where the section actually meets.
CCCID
Your 7-character California Community Colleges ID. Same across all CCCs. Required for support tickets.
CRN
Course Reference Number — the section's unique ID at the Teaching College.
AER
Authorization to Enroll Request. The form you fill out when registering. Doesn't collect SSN.
CCPG
California College Promise Grant. Per-unit fee waiver. Apply at your Home College.
Pending Person Match
Teaching College is matching your CCCID to their internal record. Normal — wait 1–3 business days.
Validated & Registered
You're fully enrolled. The good outcome.
Drop Delete Code
A Banner SIS quirk that wipes the registration record on drop. Surfaces as "Status Update Failed" — display issue, not a real failure.
Confirm Payment
The button on the post-bank CVC page that signals payment. Skipping it = card charged, college doesn't know.
Cross-enrollment
Taking a class at a different CCC (the Teaching College) while remaining a student at your Home College. The whole purpose of the CVC Exchange.
SIS
Student Information System. Each college has one — common ones are Banner, Colleague, and PeopleSoft. Your record at that college lives in their SIS.
Add code
A code instructors give out so students can add a class after registration closes. The CVC Exchange does not support add codes — once add codes are required, you must apply to the Teaching College directly.
Census date
The state-reporting "freeze" date for enrollment. Drops before census typically don't appear on transcript. Drops after census usually become a "W" grade.
Title IV
Federal financial aid programs (Pell Grant, Direct Loans). At the Teaching College, CVC students sit in a non-Title IV "shell" program — Pell can't disburse there.
Dual enrollment
A high school student also taking college courses concurrently. Requires written authorization from the high school + parent/guardian before CVC registration.
Special admit
Any non-traditional admission category (HS dual, AB540, F-1 visa, Adult Ed). Each has its own paperwork and rules; check with your Home College's A&R office.
SSO
Single Sign-On. One login that gets you into multiple systems (CVC, your Home College's portal, Canvas). Login problems are usually SSO problems and require college IT, not CVC.
TouchNet / NelNet / Cybersource
Payment processors used by various Teaching Colleges. TouchNet specifically requires the "Confirm Payment" click after the bank step — affects 23 colleges and is the #1 cause of "paid but not enrolled" tickets.
W grade
"Withdrawal" mark on your transcript. Standard outcome of dropping after the no-notation deadline. Doesn't affect GPA, but transferring colleges and aid programs may scrutinize repeated W's.
FTES
Full-Time Equivalent Student. How CCCs count enrollment for state funding. The Teaching College gets FTES credit when you take a CVC class with them — the structural reason colleges participate.
MIS reporting
California's Management Information System for CCC data. Drives funding allocation. Not student-facing — but explains some of the data the Teaching College needs (e.g., why SSN matters for CCPG matching).
AB540
California law providing in-state tuition to certain undocumented students. Apply for CCPG / Dream Act through your Home College for fee coverage.
F-1 visa
International student visa. Federal regulations restrict concurrent enrollment outside your sponsoring school. Talk to your DSO before registering through CVC.
DSO
Designated School Official. The official at your sponsoring school who handles F-1 visa paperwork. They authorize concurrent enrollment.
CCCApply
The system you use to apply to a California Community College. Find it at icangotocollege.com. Different from the CVC Exchange — CCCApply admits you to a college; CVC Exchange routes you between colleges after admission.
No-notation drop
A drop early enough in the term that nothing appears on your transcript. Usually the first 2–3 weeks of a 16-week semester (the per-section date is what counts; check the Teaching College's deadline tool).
Course Eligibility Review
When you cross-enroll into a CVC course, your Home College automatically receives a request to review whether the course fits your degree program — for financial aid packaging. This is a routine workflow, not something you need to act on. Your HC handles it on their side.
Records Research (Parchment hold)
When a transcript request can't be matched to a record at the Teaching College — almost always a name spelling mismatch. Order will sit in "Records Research" until you re-submit with the exact name on file at the TC.
Hold (Teaching College)
An account flag on the TC's side that blocks registration changes (most often blocks drops). Often financial (unpaid fees), sometimes registration-related. Banner-based colleges hit this pattern most. Clear with the TC's A&R office or cashier.
Parchment
The vendor that runs the CVC Exchange's student-facing UI and the front-line support email queue. When you email cvc.customersupport@parchment.com, Parchment's team replies first. They escalate to CVC staff for back-end actions.
SSO Proxy
The single-sign-on layer between your Home College and CVC. When your HC's login system passes your CCCID through correctly, you authenticate seamlessly. When it doesn't, you hit the "Proxy Trap" — get redirected to OpenCCC to recover or create your account.
Multiple-measures placement
When a counselor (typically at your Home College) has cleared you into a course based on your overall academic record rather than a specific completed prereq. Your CVC dashboard may show "Eligible and Approved — Skipped home and teaching approvals." That's normal — not a problem.
eTranscript
An electronic transcript send from one college to another. After your CVC class ends, the Teaching College must manually trigger an eTranscript send to your Home College's records system. Allow 4–6 weeks; if grades aren't visible after that, contact the TC's A&R office directly to nudge.
BatchApplication
The internal record CVC creates when you start an enrollment. It tracks which step you're on (Created → Consent Given → AER Submitted → Eligible and Approved → Registered → Validated). When admin staff look up your case, they're reading your BatchApplication's state.
Phase 0 / Phase 1 / Phase 2
CVC's implementation phases for each college. Phase 0 = listed as a Home College (you can authenticate from there); Phase 1 = catalog visible (you can see their courses in Course Finder); Phase 2 = live as a Teaching College (you can actually cross-enroll). Some colleges are Phase 1 only — you can browse but not register through CVC.
Open enrollment date gate
Each Teaching College sets its own date when CVC cross-enrollment opens. The "Add Class" button appears at 4 PM PST the day before that date — but registrations between 4 PM PST and midnight PST will fail. Wait until midnight PST on the open date. See §iii. window gotcha.
Instant Enrollment
CVC's term for the cross-enrollment process — registering instantly into a course at another CCC without a separate CCCApply application. The CVC Exchange uses "Cross Enrollment" and "Instant Enrollment" interchangeably. Filter for "Instant Enrollment" courses in search to see only sections that allow immediate registration.
CSU GE-Breadth
A series of courses California community college students complete to satisfy lower-division general education requirements before transferring to a CSU. CVC's search lets you filter by CSU GE-Breadth so you can find classes that count toward your transfer plan.
IGETC
Intersegmental General Education Transfer Curriculum — a series of courses to satisfy most freshman/sophomore-level GE requirements before transferring to a UC campus. Filterable in CVC search.
C-ID
California Course Identification Numbering System — a statewide standard ID for equivalent courses across CCCs. CVC search lets you find courses by your Home College's course name and find equivalents at other CCCs via C-ID mapping.
ZTC (Zero Textbook Cost)
Courses where students "pay no additional fees for learning materials (books, lab kits, etc.)" — displayed with a special badge in CVC search. Note: ZTC doesn't guarantee all materials are free; some colleges provide access through library databases. Filter for ZTC sections to reduce overall course cost.
VRC (Veterans Resource Center)
The office at your Home College that handles VA benefit certification. For CVC courses, your HC's VRC sends a Parent School Authorization Letter to the Teaching College. Talk to them before registering.
EOPS
Extended Opportunity Programs and Services — a state-funded program at your Home College providing extra academic and financial support for students from disadvantaged backgrounds. EOPS support follows you at the HC; you don't get parallel EOPS at the Teaching College for CVC courses.
Residency (in-state)
Your classification as a California resident for tuition purposes. Each college maintains its own residency record under Title 5 — there is no shared statewide database. Your Home College's classification doesn't auto-sync to the Teaching College. In-state tuition is $46/unit; non-resident is typically $300–$500+/unit. Fixing residency for a CVC course is a 2-step process: HC first, then push to TC's A&R.
Residency reclassification
When a college updates your residency status mid-term or after registration (e.g., you've now lived in CA for a year, you got AB540 approved, you went on active duty). Each college reclassifies independently. Reclassifications don't propagate from one CCC to another — you must request the change at every college that affects your enrollment.
CNTER (AB540 form)
California Nonresident Tuition Exemption Request — the form you file to claim AB540 status. Must be filed at each college separately (your Home College AND the Teaching College). The HC's approval doesn't transfer; the TC reviews CNTER fresh.
Parent School Authorization Letter (PSA)
A letter from your Home College's Veterans Resource Center (VRC) that authorizes the Teaching College to extend specific accommodations for a CVC course — most commonly for active-duty military residency exemption (Cal. Ed. Code §68075) or VA benefit certification. Request the PSA before registering through CVC; it must be in place when the TC processes your enrollment.
CA Education Code §68075
The California statute that grants in-state tuition to active-duty military service members stationed in California (and their dependents) regardless of formal residency. Process: HC's VRC certifies status, sends a Parent School Authorization Letter to the TC, TC updates residency flag.
Calbright College
A 116th California Community College — the system's online-only competency-based college. Calbright students are eligible to use CVC Exchange just like any other CCC student (Calbright as Home College, any other CCC as Teaching College). The eligibility rules apply normally (18+, 2.0 GPA or none yet, no holds, CA address).
1098-T (tax form)
The IRS tuition statement issued by colleges. For CVC courses, each Teaching College issues a separate 1098-T for tuition paid to them — not your Home College, not CVC. If you took CVC classes at three TCs, you'll need three 1098-Ts (one from each). See §vi 1098-T guide.
OpenCCC
The shared identity system that issues your CCCID and stores your name, email, and core demographics. CVC pulls from OpenCCC at every login — to change your email or name on CVC, update it at OpenCCC first (opencccapply.net); allow 24–48 hours to propagate.
Recency requirement
A program admission rule (most common in nursing) that requires prerequisite coursework to have been completed within the last N years (often 5–7). If you took the prereq longer ago, you'll need to repeat it. Repeat-for-credit requires a petition through your Home College's counselor — see §vi repeat course.
Non-resident tuition
The rate charged to students who haven't established California residency at a given college. Varies by college; commonly $300–$500/unit. Real ticket examples: $1,000 (3 units), $1,400 (3 units), $1,864 (4 units). If you're seeing this on a CVC course as a CA resident, your residency wasn't confirmed at the Teaching College — see the §vi residency guide.

Designed limits — what the system can't do, by design

Some things that look like bugs are actually intentional limits. Knowing the difference saves you from filing tickets that have no fix — and helps you plan around the real boundaries instead of fighting them.

You can't change your Home College after enrolling
drop + re-enroll required
Why this is a designed limit
The system's verification path links your CCCID to your selected Home College at enrollment time. Re-mapping that mid-stream creates more problems than it solves (financial aid emails, transcripts, MIS reporting all anchor on the original choice).
If you got the wrong one
Drop the cross-enrolled course → re-cross-enroll with the correct HC → request a refund on the original (refunds take 2–6 weeks). Annoying, but the only path. See §iii.
Out-of-state addresses aren't supported in the AER form
no workaround
Why this matters
The Authorization to Enroll form requires a California address. This was a deliberate design choice tied to residency verification, but it limits Good Neighbor (border-state) and AB540 students who may not have a CA address on file.
If this affects you
Talk to your Home College's A&R about updating your address record — some students can update to a CA address used for school correspondence. There's no way to skip this requirement at the CVC level.
2-class-per-term limit (cross-district only)
good to know
The rule
The CVC Exchange limits you to 2 classes per term across districts. If you hit the limit and see a UI error, that's the system enforcing it — not a bug.
Important nuance
The limit does NOT apply to HC↔HC enrollments (taking a class at another college within your same district). So if you're at one campus of a multi-campus district and want a class at the other campus, the 2-class limit doesn't apply.
Need more than 2?
Work with your Home College — they can authorize an exception in some cases. Or apply directly to the additional Teaching Colleges outside the Exchange via icangotocollege.com.
Federal aid (Pell, FAFSA loans) only disburses at your Home College
structural rule
Why
Title IV federal aid is administered at the institution that admitted you — your Home College. The Teaching College places you in a non-Title IV "shell" program for cross-enrollment. Federal aid can't be disbursed there.
What this means in practice
CVC courses count toward your enrollment status for aid purposes. The aid still comes from your Home College's financial aid office. You pay the Teaching College upfront with a card; the HC reimburses you per your aid package. The TC's FAFSA data is not transferred, and your TC units don't write back to your HC's aid system automatically.
After taking a CVC class at one college in a district, you can't directly register at the sibling college
designed guardrail
What happens
Example: a student takes a CVC course at Santiago Canyon College (SCC), then tries to register directly at Santa Ana College (SAC) — both in Rancho Santiago CCD. The TC's portal shows the student as already having a "CVC account" and refuses to let them register through normal admission.
Why it's designed this way
CCC districts share student records across sibling colleges to avoid double-counting for state funding (FTES). The CVC enrollment path is one record that prevents collisions with separate direct-admission records in the same district.
Two paths forward
(A) Continue using CVC Exchange for the sibling college — register through CVC selecting the new TC. This works without changes to your record. (B) If you want a direct (non-CVC) student account at the sibling college, contact the district A&R office and request your CVC record be unlinked from the original college. This is a manual process at the district level.
Not a CVC support ticket. CVC routes enrollments; the district A&R office decides how their student records are structured. Contact the district directly for the unlinking process.
Once your residency is validated, you can't change it for the term
manual fix at HC + TC
The rule
When your Home College validates your residency status at the moment of CVC registration (CA resident or not), that determination is captured in your batch application and persists for the term. The AER form cannot change it after submission.
Why each college does its own determination
California Title 5 requires every CCC to make its own residency determination for every student it enrolls. There is no shared statewide residency database. Your HC's classification doesn't auto-sync to the Teaching College's SIS — they're separate records.
Pre-payment fix
If you catch the wrong residency before paying: (1) confirm CA resident status at HC, (2) email TC's A&R with HC proof, (3) once TC updates their record, email CVC support to verify the batch application reflects the new status. Pay only after the rate corrects.
Post-payment fix
Once payment has cleared, CVC can't update the batch application's residency flag — a designed limit. Whether you get a refund or rebill is the Teaching College's decision (their bursar/student accounts office). Allow 4–6 weeks. See §vi residency guide for the full process.
Teaching Colleges can auto-drop you for non-payment without telling CVC
pay on time, every time
The risk
Each Teaching College has its own policy for dropping unpaid students — and CVC isn't always notified when they do. You may discover you've been dropped only when your class disappears from your dashboard or Canvas.
Protect yourself
Pay at the moment of registration if possible. If your CVC payment is failing, pay through the Teaching College's portal directly while CVC support investigates. The TC's clock is what matters; their auto-drop is the real risk.
If you got dropped
Email both CVC and the TC's A&R immediately. Document any payment attempts. A late re-enrollment may be possible if the section is still open and you're within their grace window — but it's case-by-case.
Transcripts back to your Home College aren't always automatic
may need follow-up
What's automatic
Grades from your CVC class flow back to your Home College's records system after the term ends — typically around 3 weeks per cvc.edu, though it can vary by college. That part works.
What's manual
Sending an official transcript to a 4-year college or employer requires the Teaching College to manually trigger an eTranscript send. If you're transferring and need a transcript, request one from the Teaching College — don't assume it's been sent automatically. Each college does this on their schedule.
HC↔HC enrollments charge local fees not visible in CVC
check your HC portal
What this is
If you take a class at another campus within your same district (HC↔HC), the CVC interface only shows tuition + enrollment fees. The local district fees (parking, student services, etc.) are charged by your HC's system but only visible in your HC's portal.
Action
After registering, log into your Home College's student portal and check the cashier/fees section. Pay any local fees there. They aren't optional.
Some colleges block HC↔HC entirely (Yuba)
apply direct
Yuba/Woodland-specific
Yuba CCD blocks HC↔HC cross-enrollment entirely. If you're at Yuba and want a class at Woodland (or vice versa), you can't use the CVC Exchange for that — apply directly through CCCApply.
For other colleges
Most allow HC↔HC. Some have quirks (Coast assesses local fees on HC↔HC; Chaffey requires manual indicator monitoring). Check the Teaching College's Confluence Special Handling page if you hit unexpected blocks.
Why this section exists — students lose hours to tickets that have no resolution because they're hitting an intentional design boundary, not a bug. Naming the boundary — "this is a designed limit, not broken" — saves the time and points at the real workaround.
